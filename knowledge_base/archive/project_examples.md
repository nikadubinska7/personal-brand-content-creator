# Prototype Projects and Applied Learning

## Purpose of This File

This file describes prototype projects, applied learning examples, and content themes that demonstrate my ability to connect AI techniques with supply chain and business process problems.

These should be framed as learning projects, prototype tools, or applied experiments.

They should not be framed as client case studies unless explicitly stated.

## Project Example 1: Sales Forecast Agent

### Description

I built an AI agent concept for sales forecasting support.

The prototype predicts next-week demand at SKU and country level and uses real business drivers such as:

- Price
- Promotions
- Inventory
- Traffic
- Holidays
- Weather
- Search behavior

The agent can also flag demand spikes and stockout risks, recommend actions such as replenishment, safety stock, or promotion review, and run what-if scenarios such as discount changes, promotion days, or traffic and inventory shifts.

### Business Problem

Demand planning often depends on multiple variables that are difficult to review manually at speed.

Teams need to understand not only what the forecast is, but also why it may change and what action could reduce risk.

### AI / Automation Angle

The prototype shows how AI can support forecasting by:

- Combining multiple demand drivers
- Detecting risks
- Explaining possible causes
- Recommending next actions
- Supporting scenario planning

### Positioning Lesson

This project shows applied thinking around AI in demand-driven businesses.

The key message is that AI forecasting is not only about prediction. It is about connecting prediction to operational decisions.

## Project Example 2: Forecast Validation and Follow-Up Automation

### Description

I created a small automation app that validates forecast timing against product availability and prepares market-specific follow-up messages.

The demo used fictional data for demonstration purposes.

### Business Problem

In real operations, forecast-related issues often happen when:

- Forecasts are submitted before agreed product availability dates
- Identifying these cases is manual
- Errors require manual follow-up with market teams
- The process becomes inconsistent, time-consuming, and error-prone

### AI / Automation Angle

The prototype demonstrates how automation can:

- Check forecast timing
- Identify mismatches
- Prepare follow-up communication
- Reduce manual review work
- Standardize the process

### Positioning Lesson

This project shows how small workflow automations can reduce operational friction.

The key message is that useful AI and automation do not always start with complex systems. Sometimes value starts with a clear rule, a repetitive check, and a structured follow-up.

- Exception detection such as late shipments, forecast errors, and stockout risks
- Supplier follow-up preparation
- Monthly KPI reporting

### Project Management

- Status reporting
- Risk and dependency tracking
- Meeting minutes and action item follow-ups

### Finance and Accounting

- Invoice processing and reconciliation support
- Expense categorization
- Month-end report automation

### Legal and Compliance

- Contract review support
- Compliance tracking and reminders

## Project Example 4: AI Project Failure Analysis

### Description

I created content analyzing why AI projects fail.

The post was based on a 2025 MIT study and discussed how most AI projects fail to deliver expected business value.

### Business Problem

Companies often assume AI failure is mainly a technical problem.

In reality, many failures come from unclear business needs, poor process fit, weak integration, or lack of adoption.

### AI / Automation Angle

The analysis connects AI success to:

- Clear business problems
- Process readiness
- Workflow integration
- User adoption
- Data and context quality
- Measurable business outcomes

### Positioning Lesson

This topic helps position my content as realistic and business-oriented.

The key message is that successful AI implementation depends on operational clarity, not only model capability.

## Project Example 7: Content Creation Assistant

### Description

This current project is a content creation assistant that uses a personal knowledge base and AI-in-supply-chain research to generate LinkedIn content ideas, briefs, and draft posts.

### Business Problem

Generic AI content often sounds repetitive and disconnected from real experience.

A personal brand content tool needs context about background, voice, examples, and strategic direction.

### AI / Automation Angle

The project uses:

- Markdown knowledge bases
- Document chunking
- Embeddings
- Local vector search
- Prompt templates
- LLM-generated content drafts
- Human review before publishing

### Positioning Lesson

This project demonstrates the ability to design a small RAG-style application that connects personal context, industry research, and structured content generation.

## General Lessons Across Projects

The content generator should use these recurring lessons:

- Start with a business problem, not a tool.
- AI becomes useful when it fits a real workflow.
- Small automations can create visible operational value.
- Forecasting and planning tools should support decisions, not just produce numbers.
- Human review remains part of the system.
- Prototype projects are useful for testing business ideas quickly.
- Clear process logic matters before automation.
- AI in supply chain should connect operational execution with strategic business impact.
