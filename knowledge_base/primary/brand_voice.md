# Brand Voice

## Voice Summary

The voice should be practical, analytical, curious, and implementation-focused.

The tone should sound like a supply chain operations professional developing AI expertise and explaining what AI means for real business workflows.

The writing should avoid overclaiming. It should not sound like an established AI agency or senior implementation expert.

## Voice Characteristics

- Practical
- Analytical
- Clear
- Curious
- Structured
- Business-focused
- Operational
- Realistic
- Focused on learning, testing, and applied use cases
- but along with above: largely human, with personal touch.

## Preferred Writing Style

Use concise LinkedIn posts with:

- A clear observation or question
- A business or supply chain problem
- A practical AI angle
- A concrete operational example
- A short lesson or reflection
- A soft invitation to discuss

## Writing Rhythm and Structure

The post should feel like a practical reflection, not a polished consulting article.

Preferred structure:

1. Start with a simple, direct observation.
2. Use short paragraphs with enough spacing to make the post easy to read.
3. Explain the operational problem before introducing the AI angle.
4. Connect the idea to workflows, recurring tasks, checks, reporting, follow-ups, document handling, or decision-making.
5. Add a practical reflection about what this means for people, teams, or business processes.
6. End with a thoughtful reflection or soft question, not a generic call to action.

Preferred sentence style:

- Simple and human.
- Clear but not overly polished.
- Reflective, as if explaining what I noticed while learning, building, or connecting AI to real operations.
- Use first person when it is natural: "I looked back...", "I have been exploring...", "This made me think...".
- Use practical phrases such as "what really matters", "in practice", "recurring tasks", "real problems", "mental space", "better decision-making", and "how the workflow could look".

Avoid corporate article language such as:

- "In the realm of..."
- "AI can be transformative"
- "hinges on one critical factor"
- "fosters a culture of accountability"
- "successful implementation"
- "drive value"
- "unlock potential"
- "let's discuss"

## What the Writing Should Do

The content should:

- Show growing expertise without pretending to know everything
- Demonstrate logical thinking
- Connect AI tools to business processes
- Show awareness of AI trends in business
- Explain AI through supply chain and operations examples
- Build credibility through practical analysis and prototype work
- Attract employers or future clients by showing capability and direction

## What to Avoid

Avoid:

- “I help businesses…” claims
- Overclaiming implementation experience
- Sounding like an established AI consulting firm
- Generic phrases like “AI is changing everything”
- Empty motivational language
- Too many buzzwords
- Claims without operational examples
- Overpromising AI results
- Presenting opinions as absolute truth

## Phrases to Avoid

Avoid phrases like:

- “I help companies implement AI…”
- “My clients achieve…”
- “We transform your supply chain with AI…”
- “As an AI implementation expert…”
- “Proven AI transformation framework…”
- “Guaranteed results…”
